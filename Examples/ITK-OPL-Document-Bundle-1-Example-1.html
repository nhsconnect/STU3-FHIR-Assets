History

Presenting complaint or issue Following a stroke Mr. Linacre experienced acquired swallowing difficulties.
History of each presenting complaint or issue Mr. Linacre attended the community nutrition and dietetics outpatient clinic for review of feeding. During a recent admission to hospital Mr. Linacre was established on PEG tube feeding. The feeding tube insitu is a 15French PEG tube placed 05/04/17.
History since last contact Mr. Linacre's feeding regimen being: 1000mls Energy Multifibre Feed at 100mls/hours for 10 hours (9am-7pm) with 1400mls water given as divided flushes (e.g. 10x140mls) throughout the day e.g. before and after feed and with medications. His weight is stable. Mr. Linacre has tolerated the regimen well.

Allergies and adverse reactions

Allergy/adverse reaction No known allergies or adverse reactions

Examination findings

Examination findings Weight 80kg, Height 175cm, BMI 45.71kg/m2

Medications and medical devices

Medication name Energy Multifibre feed
Route Via PEG tube
Dose 100ml
Medication frequency Mr. Linacre needs 100ml/day of energy fibre feed given via PEG tube over 10hrs (10am-7pm)
Additional instructions Please prescribe 28 x 100ml bags per month. Prescription to be sent directly to the feed company who will deliver direct to patient.
Medication name Water
Route Flushes via PEG tube
Dose 1400mls
Medication frequency Divided flushes (e.g. 10x140mls) throughout the day e.g. before and after feed and with medications.
Medical devices Mr. Linacre has been supplied with a feeding pump.

Diagnoses

Diagnosis Stroke

Problems and issues

Problems and Issues Acquired swallowing difficulties

Clinical summary

Clinical Summary
The estimated nutritional requirements for Mr. Linacre are Energy 1500kcla/day, Protein 80g/day, Fluid 2400mls/day.
Mr. Linacre is tolerating his feed and fluid flushes well as per his feeding regimen and he reports being compliant with his medication.
Mr. Linacre's peg site has healed and was clean and dry and exposed on assessment.
Mr. Linacre's bowels are opening daily (with no bowel meds), all pressure areas are intact and his weight is stable.

Procedures

Procedure Review of feeding regimen
Procedure PEG
